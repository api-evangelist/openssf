<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">By Stacey Potter and Eddie Knight</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The</span><a href="https://securityslam.com/"> <span style="font-weight: 400;">2026 Security Slam</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> has officially concluded, and we couldn&#8217;t be more proud of the progress made across the open source ecosystem. From automated baseline evaluations to comprehensive threat modeling, our participating projects and contributors have taken significant steps to “secure open source at the source.&#8221;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-10826 size-large" height="683" src="https://openssf.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/55177209689_c5e9be87b3_k-1024x683.jpg" width="1024" />The event culminated in an</span><a href="https://kccnceu2026.sched.com/event/2GrIc/security-slam-awards-ceremony"> <span style="font-weight: 400;">Awards Ceremony</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> at KubeCon + CloudNativeCon Europe 2026, where we recognized the following outstanding achievements:</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Top Finishers: 5-Badge Projects</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">These projects demonstrated exceptional commitment by earning all five available badges: </span><b>Cleaner, Chronicler, Inspector, Mechanizer, and Defender</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://securityslam.com/slam26"><b>Privateer</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">: A massive congratulations to the team and </span><b>Jason Meridth</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, who earned the first </span><b>Security Champion</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge this year — in addition to acting as a Slam Advisor for other projects.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://securityslam.com/slam26"><b>CloudNativePG</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">: Huge kudos to </span><b>Gabriele Bartolini</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> for his leadership in achieving all five objectives and earning the </span><b>Security Champion</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> designation.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://securityslam.com/slam26"><b>Meshery</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">: A stellar team effort! </span><b>Sangram Rath</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> earned a </span><b>Security Champion</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge for his work on four objectives, while </span><b>Jonathan Abdiel</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> earned a </span><b>Security Advocate</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge.</span></li>
</ul>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">High Achievers: 4-Badge Projects</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Completing four of the five rigorous objectives is a significant feat. These projects are well on their way to total baseline compliance.</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://securityslam.com/slam26"><b>Kyverno</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">: Earned the </span><b>Cleaner, Chronicler, Mechanizer, and Inspector</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badges. </span><b>Shuting Zhao</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> was instrumental in this success, earning a </span><b>Security Champion</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge for her efforts.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://securityslam.com/slam26"><b>Metal3</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">: Also secured the </span><b>Cleaner, Chronicler, Mechanizer, and Inspector</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badges. </span><b>Tuomo Tanskanen</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">’s leadership earned him the </span><b>Security Champion</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge.</span></li>
</ul>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Making an Impact: Notable Contributions</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Security is a journey, and every objective completed makes the ecosystem safer.</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://securityslam.com/slam26"><b>Repository Service for TUF (RSTUF)</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">: Earned the </span><b>Cleaner</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge with key contributions from </span><b>Camila Vilarinho</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, our newest </span><b>Security Advocate</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://securityslam.com/slam26"><b>Minder</b></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">: Earned the </span><b>Cleaner</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge thanks to the hard work of </span><b>Evan Anderson</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, who also earned a </span><b>Security Advocate</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge.</span></li>
</ul>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our Guiding Stars: Security Advisors</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Special recognition goes to our </span><b>Security Advisors</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">. These individuals are the backbone of the event, having created the &#8220;Slam Library&#8221; content and provided direct support to the projects listed above. For their leadership, they have been awarded the </span><b>Security Slam 2026 Advisor</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> badge:</span></p>
<ul>

</ul>
