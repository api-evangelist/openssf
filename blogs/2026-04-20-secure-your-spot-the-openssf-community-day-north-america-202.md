<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The </span><a href="https://events.linuxfoundation.org/openssf-community-day-north-america/program/schedule/?__hstc=14087400.fa44f7eb60e3fd9accfe972a6d7ff2ff.1700078297599.1775849770027.1776113531489.1366&amp;__hssc=14087400.3.1776113531489&amp;__hsfp=3ffd2d8aa4bb939356b799c366b5bd58"><span style="font-weight: 400;">2026 OpenSSF Community Day North America agenda is live</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, and we invite the open source community to join us on Thursday, May 21, in Minneapolis, MN. Co-located with </span><a href="https://openssf.org/event/open-source-summit-north-america-2/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Open Source Summit North America</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, this event will serve as a collaborative space for maintainers, security researchers, and industry leaders to discuss the state and future of software supply chain security. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This year’s agenda will feature a balance of technical deep dives into SBOMs and digital signatures alongside forward-looking discussions on post-quantum cryptography and AI-driven remediation.There will be much to discuss during the event. Here’s what you’ll find on the schedule:</span></p>
<h3><b>Keynote Sessions and Morning Programming</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The day will begin with </span><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I46s"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Opening Remarks</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> from Steve Fernandez, OpenSSF General Manager, welcoming everybody and sharing exciting news and things you do not want to miss from the OpenSSF community.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I44z/keynote-anatomy-of-a-phishing-campaign-mike-fiedler-python-software-foundation?iframe=yes&amp;w=&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;<span class="a_GcMg font-feature-liga-off font-feature-clig-off font-feature-calt-off text-decoration-none text-strikethrough-none">Keynote: Anatomy of a Phishing Campaign</span>&#8220;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Mike Fiedler, Python Software Foundation, will provide a technical look at reducing the reliance on long-lived secrets in the release process.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I452/keynote-bear-ing-fruit-a-year-of-learning-mentorship-and-community-building-in-open-source-security-marcela-melara-research-scientist-intel-corporation-yesenia-yser-senior-security-program-manager-microsoft?iframe=yes&amp;w=&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;BEAR-ing Fruit: A Year of Learning, Mentorship, and Community Building in Open Source Security&#8221;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Marcela Melara, BEAR Working Group &amp; Yesenia Yser, Microsoft. Yesenia will offer a retrospective on a year of security mentorship and the growth of the open source security community.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I455/the-architecture-of-accountability-transparency-in-software-hayden-blauzvern-google?iframe=yes&amp;w=&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;The Architecture of Accountability: Transparency in Software&#8221;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Hayden Blauzvern, Google. Hear from Hayden as this session will examine the structural requirements for maintaining transparency in software ecosystems.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Following the morning break, sessions will shift toward Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) and repository automation.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I458/gemara-the-grc-architecture-you-didnt-know-you-built-hannah-braswell-jennifer-power-red-hat?iframe=yes&amp;w=100%&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;Gemara: The GRC Architecture You Didn’t Know You Built&#8221;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Hannah Braswell &amp; Jennifer Power, Red Hat; and Eddie Knight &#8212; This talk will explore existing architectural patterns that support governance and compliance.</span></p>
<h3><b>Supply Chain Transparency and Artifact Security</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The afternoon sessions will provide deep dives into Software Bills of Materials (SBOMs), digital signatures, and vulnerability management.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I45K/petra-sboms-without-oversharing-for-confidential-supply-chain-transparency-eman-abu-ishgair-purdue-university-marcela-melara-intel-corporation?iframe=yes&amp;w=&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;Petra: SBOMs Without Oversharing for Confidential Supply Chain Transparency&#8221;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Eman Abu Ishgair, Purdue University &amp; Marcela Melara, BEAR Working Group This presentation will cover methods for sharing SBOM data without compromising sensitive information.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I45c/verification-toward-applying-slsa-in-automotive-ivi-software-development-yuta-kiyoumi-takashi-ninjouji-honda-motor-co-ltd?iframe=yes&amp;w=&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;Verification Toward Applying SLSA in Automotive IVI Software Development&#8221;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Yuta Kiyoumi &amp; Takashi Ninjouji, Honda Motor Co., Ltd. This talk will focus on the application of SLSA frameworks within the automotive software sector.</span></p>
<h3><b>Emerging Technologies: AI and Quantum Readiness</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The final block of the day will explore how security must evolve to meet the challenges of artificial intelligence and quantum computing.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I45i/quantum-proofing-sigstore-a-tale-of-three-approaches-kevin-conner-red-hat?iframe=yes&amp;w=&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;Quantum Proofing Sigstore: A Tale of Three Approaches&#8221;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Kevin Conner, Red Hat. The session will detail strategies for preparing Sigstore for a post-quantum cryptographic landscape.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://openssfcdna2026.sched.com/event/2I45l/ai-as-security-orchestrator-an-introduction-to-darnit-michael-lieberman-kusari?iframe=yes&amp;w=&amp;sidebar=yes&amp;bg=no"><span style="font-weight: 400;">&#8220;AI as Security Orchestrator: An Introduction To Darnit&#8221;</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Michael Lieberman, Kusari. This talk will introduce Darnit and its role in AI-driven security orchestration.</span></p>
